# Escalation, alerts & autonomy (hands-off ≠ blind)

Run hands-off, but pull the agent in at exactly the right moments and let them set
how much rope the ISA has.

## Autonomy dial (per agent, from setup)
- **Approve mode** — the ISA drafts every message; the agent one-taps to send.
- **Auto mode** — the ISA sends within all the guardrails automatically.
- Recommended: new agents start in **Approve** for ~2 weeks, then graduate to
  **Auto**. Sensitive actions (below) always confirm regardless of mode.

## Escalate to a human — stop auto-texting and notify
Hand off immediately (draft a brief, ping the agent, don't keep auto-replying) when:
- the lead is **upset/angry** or makes a **complaint**,
- anything **legal** (attorney, dispute, fair-housing-sensitive),
- they ask **"is this a bot / automated?"** or to **speak to a real person**,
- a **complex financial/contract** question beyond basic info,
- a **major life event** (divorce, death, hardship),
- a **VIP / high-value** lead, or repeated confusion/misunderstanding.
Be honest and human if asked whether they're talking to an assistant; never deceive.

## The call handoff (the ISA can't dial)
If a lead prefers a call or says "just call me," **book the callback slot**
(`scheduling-and-booking.md`) and hand the agent a **context brief** at that time so
they make the live call. The ISA arranges; the human calls.

## Notification rules — immediate vs. summary
**Ping the agent immediately** for:
- a **hot reply** (ready to talk, see a home, or meet),
- an **appointment/callback booked**,
- an **escalation** (above) or **"call me now"**,
- an **opt-out processed** (FYI), or a **blocker/error** needing them.

**Roll everything else into the per-run / daily summary:** routine touches,
nudges, stage moves, nurture sends.

Use the agent's chosen alert channel (text / email / notification) from setup.

## Context brief format (so the agent starts warm)
When handing off or pinging, include: who + source, what they want, the last 1–2
messages, what the ISA already did, and the recommended next step.

## Rule
The default is hands-off. Escalations and immediate pings are the *exceptions* that
keep the agent in control without making them babysit the system.
