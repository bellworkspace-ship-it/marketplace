# Bailed appointments & ghosted meetings

Two high-value recovery cases. Don't let a set appointment or a completed meeting die
— re-engage to rebook or to find what the lead actually wants.

## Case 1 — Appointment Set, but they no-showed / bailed
**Detect:** the appointment time has passed with no completed outcome, or it's marked
no-show/canceled, or they stopped replying before it.

**Re-engage (warm, no guilt):**
> "Hey {first} — looks like we missed each other earlier. No worries at all. Want me
> to grab another time this week? I've got {two slots}."

- Offer two concrete new times (`scheduling-and-booking.md`) and rebook on a yes.
- If no reply after ~2–3 spaced touches, move them back to **Spoke With Customer**
  and keep nurturing toward a new appointment. Don't keep hammering.
- Log the no-show and the re-engagement.

## Case 2 — Met With Customer, then went quiet
**Detect:** a meeting happened but there's been no meaningful activity for ~3–7+ days
and they haven't advanced to an active client.

**Re-engage to uncover the need, then go find it:**
> "Hi {first} — great meeting you. Quick gut check so I only send what actually
> fits: what's most important — {price / area / timing / a must-have}? I'll line up
> the best options."

- Learn timeline, must-haves, price, and blockers.
- Then **go find it** — pull real matching listings or an updated value via the
  agent's MLS/valuation tools (`mls-and-valuation-data.md`) — and bring it back with a
  next-step ask (another showing, a call, a listing consult).
- Advance toward an active client; if they truly go cold, drop to **Nurture** with a
  slower value cadence (don't lose them).

## Rules
- Always warm and low-pressure — a bail is not a no.
- Lead every recovery touch with value or a real next step, never "just checking in."
- Respect opt-outs and caps; escalate to the agent if the lead is upset or it's
  sensitive (`escalation-alerts-autonomy.md`).
