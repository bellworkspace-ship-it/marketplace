# Opt-out & compliance (do this exactly)

This is the most important guardrail in the system. When a lead wants out, act
immediately and completely. Used by both the First Responder and the existing-lead
nurture skills.

## What counts as an opt-out
Any clear signal to stop, including: "stop", "unsubscribe", "remove me", "take me
off your list", "do not contact", "quit texting me", "not interested", "leave me
alone", or an unambiguous request to stop hearing from the agent. When in doubt,
treat it as an opt-out — never risk re-contacting someone who asked to stop.

## The opt-out flow (run every step, in order)
1. **Stop immediately.** Cancel any queued/next touches for this lead. Do not send
   another marketing message.
2. **Mark the channel as Do Not Contact.** Mark the **phone number and/or email**
   that opted out so it is never texted/emailed again (set its status to
   opted-out / Do Not Contact in FUB). A number on the Do Not Call list must never
   be texted.
3. **Apply the `Do Not Contact` tag** to the contact.
4. **Move the lead to the Fishing Pond.** Reassign the contact to the Fishing Pond
   (the FUB Pond configured in setup). Reassigning to a pond also adds the pond
   name as a tag.
5. **Set the stage** appropriately (e.g., Trash / closed-lost per the account's
   stages) so it leaves the active pipeline.
6. **Log it.** Add a note recording the opt-out (what they said, date, channel) and
   record it in the team learning log as an `opt_out` outcome.
7. **Confirm politely only if appropriate.** If a brief, compliant confirmation is
   warranted (e.g. "You're all set — I won't reach out again. Take care."), send it
   once; otherwise stay silent. Never argue or try to save the opt-out.

## General compliance posture
- **Never text a Do Not Call / opted-out number**, ever.
- **Honor opt-outs across channels** — if they opt out of text, don't switch to
  email to get around it (unless they clearly only objected to one channel).
- **Older leads:** lead with genuine value and keep frequency low; respect that
  consent may be stale. Be easy to opt out of.
- **Quiet hours:** never message outside the agent's configured sending hours.
- **Frequency caps:** never exceed one automated message per lead per run, and
  respect the cadence spacing — do not pile on.
- **Sensitive situations** (complaint, legal, distress, major life event): hold for
  the agent rather than auto-replying.

## Why this matters
Responsible opt-out handling keeps the agent compliant, protects the brokerage's
sending reputation, and keeps the database clean. Treat it as non-negotiable.
